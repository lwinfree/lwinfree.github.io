Lilly Winfree

I'm an ex-neuroscience researcher turned science analyst, communicator, and open science advocate. I currently work for the Monarch Initiative, an international consortium focused on integrating multi-species biological data and supporting open science efforts, where I analyze our data, present at conferences, and write blogs. I am a founding member of the Portland Women in Science organization and acting news editor on the communications committee. I have co-organized conferences such as Cascadia R Conf, a local programming conference with hands-on educational workshops, and Science Hack Day Portland, a 36-hour open source event focused on making fun things with science!

I'm a classically trained neuroscience researcher, and have my PhD from Oregon Health and Science University. While in grad school, I found my passions for open science, teaching, data analysis, and advocacy (especially for women in science!).
